Rules Cheatsheet

Key differences between BECMI D&D (this game) and modern systems like D&D 5e. If you've played 5e, Pathfinder, or similar — read this first.

The Most Important Differences
Armor Class goes DOWN. AC 2 is better than AC 7. Plate Mail = AC 3. A naked person = AC 9. The lower your AC, the harder you are to hit.
0 HP = Dead. No death saving throws. No stabilization at −1. When you hit 0, you are dead. Some DMs use an optional −10 rule; ask before the session.
The attack table on your character sheet does all the math for you. Find your target's AC in the left column, read the "Equipped" column — that's the minimum roll you need on a d20 to hit. Natural 1 always misses. Natural 20 always hits.
Other games (5e / PF2)
AC goes up. AC 18 is harder to hit than AC 12. You roll d20 + attack bonus vs. their AC. Lower HP = unconscious with death saves.
This game (BECMI D&D)
AC goes down. AC 2 is harder to hit than AC 7. Roll d20 vs. a target number (from your sheet). 0 HP = dead, full stop.
Other games
Saving throws are tied to ability scores (STR save, DEX save, CON save…). Proficiency bonus adds to them.
This game
Five fixed categories: Death Ray, Wands, Paralysis, Breath, Spells. Each has a target number on your sheet. Roll d20 equal or higher to succeed.
Other games
Spells are "slots" — you can cast any memorized spell using a slot. Cantrips are free. Short rest may recover slots.
This game
Spells are memorized individually and forgotten when cast. No cantrips. No short rest recovery. Spellcasters have very few spells per day.
Other games
Advantage/Disadvantage: roll 2d20, take higher or lower. Proficiency Bonus on attacks, saves, and skills.
This game
Neither exists. Situational modifiers are flat +/− numbers decided by the DM. There is no proficiency bonus — your class and level determine what you can do.
Attacking & THAC0

How to Attack

  1. Find the target's AC on your attack table (left column).
  2. Read the Equipped column — that's your minimum roll.
  3. Roll d20. Equal or higher = HIT.
  4. Natural 1 = always miss. Natural 20 = always hit.
  5. On a hit, roll weapon damage + STR bonus (melee) or DEX bonus (ranged) + magic weapon bonus.
Example: Strongheart has THAC0 12 equipped. Target is AC 3. His table shows he needs a 9 or higher. He rolls a 14 — hit!

What is THAC0?

"To Hit Armor Class 0." It's the roll you'd need to hit an enemy with AC 0 (the hardest to hit). Your sheet pre-calculates every AC for you — you never need to do the math yourself. The "Base" column ignores equipment bonuses; "Equipped" is what you actually roll.

What STR Does

  • +/− to melee attack rolls
  • +/− to melee and thrown weapon damage
  • Affects ability to force open doors (1–2 in 6 base chance)
  • No effect on ranged weapon damage

What DEX Does

  • +/− to missile attack rolls only
  • Adjusts Armor Class (DEX 13+ lowers your AC)
  • Optional: DEX bonus applies to individual initiative
  • No effect on melee attack rolls
Death & Healing
You die at 0 HP. There is no "unconscious and stable." There are no death saving throws. If you reach 0, your character is dead. The party must decide whether to flee or press on without you.

Healing Options

  • Cleric cure spells — the primary source; limited per day
  • Full rest (8+ hours) — some DMs allow 1d3 HP recovery; this is optional — confirm with the DM
  • Cure Light Wounds — restores 1d6+1 HP; each use consumes the spell for the day
  • Potions of Healing — typically 1d6+1 HP; check item description
There are NO "short rests," "hit dice spending," or "Second Wind" equivalents. Manage your HP carefully.

Resurrection

  • Raise Dead (5th-level Cleric spell) can revive a dead character if cast within Constitution score in days
  • The character returns with 1 HP and needs a full week of rest
  • Each resurrection may permanently reduce CON by 1 (DM's option)
  • Characters slain by energy drain or certain weapons (e.g., Soulcleaver) cannot be raised
  • No Revivify at low cost — Raise Dead is a high-level, rare spell

Level Drain (Energy Drain)

  • Certain undead (Wights, Wraiths, Vampires) and some weapons drain actual experience levels
  • You lose all benefits of the drained level: HP, spells, abilities, to-hit bonus
  • A 1st-level character drained to 0 levels is dead
  • There is no save. Only magic items or special conditions can protect against it
Grimsword's sword drains 1 level on a hit. No save. Handle with care.
Spells & Magic

How Memorization Works

  • Spellcasters choose which spells to memorize each morning (8 hours rest + study)
  • When a spell is cast, it is forgotten — gone until re-memorized the next day
  • You can memorize the same spell twice to cast it twice
  • Your sheet shows spell slots as inputs — cross off spells as you cast them
No cantrips. No at-will spells. Every casting matters.

Magic-User vs. Cleric

  • Magic-Users keep a spellbook; can only cast what they've found and copied; very few spells per day at low levels
  • Clerics can access any cleric spell of their level — no spellbook — but still limited daily slots
  • Elves use the MU spell list; fewer/lower-level slots than a pure MU

Important Spell Rules

  • No "concentration" mechanic — spells run their listed duration automatically
  • Casting in melee: the DM may require you not to move; getting hit during casting may disrupt the spell
  • Scrolls can be used by the appropriate class; 10% chance of backfire for thieves using MU scrolls
  • A MU's Intelligence score determines the maximum level of spells they can learn

Saving vs. Spells

  • The target rolls a save vs. Spells/Rods/Staves to resist most spells
  • A successful save often means half damage (e.g., fireball) or no effect (e.g., charm, sleep)
  • Read the spell description — it specifies the category and outcome
  • Some spells (Magic Missile, Cloudkill) allow no save
Combat Round Sequence (10 seconds)

Order of Play Each Round

  1. Morale (monsters only)
    The DM checks whether monsters flee or fight on.
  2. Initiative
    Each side rolls 1d6. Higher roll acts first. Ties = simultaneous.
  3. Missile Attacks
    Archers, slingers, and thrown weapon users fire. Targets can't dodge.
  4. Spells
    Spellcasters declare and resolve spells. Getting hit before your turn may ruin the spell.
  5. Movement
    Characters move up to their full movement rate, or half movement and still attack.
  6. Melee Attacks
    Hand-to-hand attacks resolved. Roll to hit, then roll damage.
The side that lost initiative also performs all 6 steps, just after the winning side. Both sides may attack in the same round.

Retreating From Melee

  • Fighting Withdrawal: move up to half speed backward; enemy does not get a free attack
  • Full Retreat: move full speed away; enemy gets a free attack at +2; you lose your attack for the round
  • Running characters do NOT get to attack the fleeing character if they are already in melee with someone else
Class & Race Special Rules

⚔ Fighter / Sweep Attack

Fighters (and demi-human fighters) get 1 attack per level against creatures with 1 HD or fewer in a single round. Example: a Level 6 Fighter attacks 6 goblins in one round. Against creatures with more than 1 HD: 1 attack per round.

This is a massive advantage vs. mobs. Keep it in mind against skeletons, kobolds, and goblins.

🗡 Thief / Backstab

Requires: approaching unseen and attacking with a one-handed melee weapon.

  • +4 to attack roll
  • ×2 damage at levels 1–4
  • ×3 damage at levels 5–8 (Molliver & Zarak)
  • ×4 damage at levels 9–12
Multiply the weapon damage dice, THEN add STR and magic bonuses. Does not work with missile weapons.

🔮 Thief / Skills

Thief skills are percentage rolls (roll d100, equal or under = success). Your sheet lists the exact percentages. Notable exceptions:

  • Hear Noise: roll 1–3 on d6 (or 1–4 at higher levels)
  • Climb Walls: very high % but a failed roll means a fall

☩ Cleric / Turn Undead

Roll 2d6 and compare to the Turn Undead table on your sheet.

  • Number: roll equal or higher to turn 2d6 HD of undead
  • T: automatic turn (2d6 HD flee)
  • D: automatic destroy (3d6 HD destroyed)
Chaotic (evil) clerics CONTROL rather than turn or destroy undead.

🧝 Elf

  • Can fight and cast spells — but not in the same round
  • Immune to Ghoul paralysis
  • Detects secret/hidden doors on a 1–2 in 6 when passing
  • Infravision 60'
  • Max level: 10 (Peralay is at max)
  • Spells use the MU list; fewer slots than a pure MU of the same level

⛏ Dwarf

  • Detects sloping passages, new construction, shifting walls (1–2 in 6 when searching)
  • Infravision 60'
  • Takes half damage from damage-causing spells at high level (1,400,000 XP)
  • Excellent saves — see Dwarf save table, notably better vs. magic than fighters
  • Max level: 12; uses Fighter attack table

🌿 Halfling

  • Outdoors: 90% chance to be unseen (motionless in natural cover)
  • Indoors: 33% chance to hide motionless in dim light
  • +1 to missile attack rolls; −2 AC vs. creatures larger than man-size
  • Half damage from dragon breath (and spells at 900,000 XP)
  • Excellent saves — equal to Dwarves; best of any class at their level
  • Max level: 8; uses Fighter attack table

📜 Magic-User

  • Weakest fighter — no armor, one dagger (no shield)
  • Fragile at low HP; protect them in the back of the party
  • THAC0 improves very slowly (stays at 19 through level 10)
  • Use spells strategically — you have very few per day
  • At high levels, become exponentially more powerful

⚒ Weapons: What Clerics Can't Use

Clerics cannot use edged or pointed weapons — no swords, axes, daggers, spears, arrows.

Clerics can use: maces, war hammers, clubs, staves, flails, and slings.

Mercion (Mace +2) and Zargash (Mace +1, Snake Staff) follow this restriction. Their weapons deal 1d6 damage.
